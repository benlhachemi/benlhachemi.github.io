## 🖼️ Background

### Affiliate Marketer (2020 → 2023)
I started my career in affiliate marketing at [Ogads](https://ogads.com). To compete, I built my own tools: landing pages, tracking systems, browser fingerprinting scripts, and automation pipelines.

That's when I realized the tools were more interesting to me than the marketing. I wasn't thinking about traffic — I was thinking about how to build better software.

### Independent Builder (2023 → 2026)

I spent three years building products on my own. I launched more than [20 projects](/projects) — ranging from small experiments to full production applications across SaaS, desktop, AI, and automation.

None of them became a real business. Some got users but didn't convert. Some I shut down after a few weeks. Most taught me something I couldn't have learned any other way.

What I did learn: how to design systems, make product decisions under uncertainty, deploy and monitor real applications, and ship things that actually work. I also learned that building is only half the picture — distribution is an entirely separate skill, and trying to do both alone is genuinely hard.

### Now: Looking for a Team

I've decided I want to focus on what I'm actually good at: building. I'd rather be the engineer inside a team with a clear product direction than try to do everything alone again.

I'm actively looking for my first professional role. I know I'm coming in without team experience, and I'm not pretending otherwise. What I do have is three years of shipping real code, a high tolerance for ambiguity, and a strong motivation to prove myself in a collaborative setting.

## 🧠 My Engineering Philosophy

- **Build → Iterate → Loop:** Start simple, get it working, then improve. I've done this cycle enough times that it's instinct now.

- **Clean architecture by default:** Strong folder structures, single-responsibility components, self-documenting code. I hold this standard even when working alone — it's the only way to stay sane across 20 projects.

- **UI/UX is a core feature:** Micro-interactions, accessibility, and polish are half the product. I've shipped enough things to know users feel the difference even when they can't name it.

- **Failure is data:** Most of my projects didn't work commercially. Each one taught me something I couldn't have gotten from a course.

- **AI as a tool, not a shortcut:** I use AI coding tools to move faster. I still write and understand the code myself.

## 🔁 My Workflow

1. Understand the problem deeply before writing any code
2. Explore multiple solutions
3. Compare by cost, feasibility, and complexity
4. Build and ship an MVP
5. Collect feedback and usage data
6. Iterate based on what I learn
